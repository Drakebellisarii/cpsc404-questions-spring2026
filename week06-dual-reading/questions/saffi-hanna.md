Kousen's Chapter 4 argues employees should send two messages — "I got this" and "I got your back" — to build trust with a single manager. In open source (Chapter 8), there's no single boss, yet contributors still need to earn trust and demonstrate reliability to maintainers and the community. How do the dynamics of "I got this" and "I got your back" change when the manager-employee relationship is replaced by the decentralized, volunteer-driven structure of an open source project?
